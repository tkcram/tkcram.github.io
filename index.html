RGB Values

The letters "RGB" stand for red, green, and blue. When people make colors appear on your computer screen, they are mixing red, green, and blue together, just like how you mix paint together to get different colors. We call these "RGB values."
Like we learned in color theory, there are a few different types of color wheels. There is a color wheel that is used by artists and one that is used digitally. The artist's color wheel uses primary colors, which are red, blue, and yellow. A digital color wheel uses red, green, and blue, because light behaves differently on a computer screen.
Computers also see colors differently than humans do. While we see colors as a reflection of light, computers see colors as numbers. The numbers in the RGB values tell the computer how much red, green, and blue to mix.

Digital pictures are made up of pixels. You might be familiar with pixels if you've ever played an old video game, or Minecraft, where the pixels are very large.

In a digital photo, each pixel is just one color. Many pixels together will blend to create a more complex color.

Suggested Activity

Split into groups of 3 or 4. Have each group member pick a color using this generator. Using only red, green, and blue paint, try to mix your color!
Once everyone has their color, create a piece of art using your colors. You could even try making pixel art!
